# Homepage Audience Archetypes

## 1. Curious beginner

- **Likely device:** Phone, often from Instagram, text, referral, or event mention.
- **Emotional state:** Interested but unsure; may feel intimidated by music language.
- **Main question:** “Can I start here even if I am new?”
- **Objections/friction:** Fear of not being talented enough, unclear cost, uncertainty about schedule and level.
- **Proof needed:** Beginner-friendly language, Foundations of Musicianship, workshop examples, supportive tone.
- **Best CTA:** “Start with Foundations” or “Get Event Notifications.”
- **Content needed:** Short explanation of what beginners learn, expected commitment, cost/status, next available date.
- **Interaction that may help:** Find Your Path panel that reveals beginner-specific reassurance and next step.
- **Desired feeling:** “I can begin without already being an expert.”

## 2. Returning adult musician

- **Likely device:** Phone or laptop; may browse quietly before taking action.
- **Emotional state:** Nostalgic, cautious, possibly self-conscious.
- **Main question:** “Is there a welcoming way back into music?”
- **Objections/friction:** Rustiness, time constraints, fear of judgment, unclear fit with younger students.
- **Proof needed:** Adult-specific language, flexible entry points, community support, no shame around starting again.
- **Best CTA:** “Find Your Path” → “Return to Music.”
- **Content needed:** Re-entry framing, Foundations fit, community singing fit, possible private/group options.
- **Interaction that may help:** Path selector option: “I used to make music and want to return.”
- **Desired feeling:** “This is a serious but kind place to come back.”

## 3. Community singer

- **Likely device:** Phone after hearing about Vanguard Voices or seeing social content.
- **Emotional state:** Excited, socially motivated, curious about standards and belonging.
- **Main question:** “Can I sing with DSG, and what is expected?”
- **Objections/friction:** Audition anxiety, schedule uncertainty, fear of not being advanced enough.
- **Proof needed:** Vanguard Voices purpose, rehearsal/performance tone, leadership/care balance, real photo/video proof.
- **Best CTA:** “Explore Vanguard Voices” or “Get Vanguard Voices Updates.”
- **Content needed:** Who it is for, expected commitment, join/audition process, upcoming performance or notification form.
- **Interaction that may help:** Vanguard Voices proof module with join options and FAQ accordion.
- **Desired feeling:** “This chorus has standards, but I could belong here.”

## 4. Emerging artist/songwriter

- **Likely device:** Phone; sometimes laptop if reviewing services.
- **Emotional state:** Ambitious but resource-constrained; may be skeptical of nonprofit promises.
- **Main question:** “Can DSG help me turn ideas into real recordings or performances?”
- **Objections/friction:** Cost, quality concerns, unclear eligibility, fear of gatekeeping.
- **Proof needed:** Mobile recording opportunity, demos/content capture language, pathway to visibility, public outcomes.
- **Best CTA:** “Explore Recording Opportunity” or “Get Recording Updates.”
- **Content needed:** What recording support includes, current availability, eligibility, examples if approved.
- **Interaction that may help:** Program accordion comparing Foundations, Recording, and Vanguard Voices by outcome.
- **Desired feeling:** “DSG understands the gap between talent and proof.”

## 5. Donor

- **Likely device:** Phone after referral/social post or desktop from email.
- **Emotional state:** Supportive but needs confidence and specificity.
- **Main question:** “What will my gift make possible?”
- **Objections/friction:** Lack of impact proof, uncertainty about nonprofit status, third-party donation trust.
- **Proof needed:** 501(c)(3) identity, tangible gift levels, program outcomes, Zeffy link, public proof through Vanguard Voices.
- **Best CTA:** “Sponsor Opportunity.”
- **Content needed:** Donation levels, what each level supports, nonprofit reassurance, no exaggerated claims.
- **Interaction that may help:** Donor impact selector with $25/$50/$100/custom outcomes.
- **Desired feeling:** “My gift creates a concrete music opportunity.”

## 6. Partner / school / community organization

- **Likely device:** Desktop or tablet during planning; phone after referral.
- **Emotional state:** Evaluative, logistical, mission-aligned but practical.
- **Main question:** “Can DSG bring a credible music program to our community?”
- **Objections/friction:** Scheduling, cost, staffing, safeguarding, outcomes, fit for audience.
- **Proof needed:** Clear program menu, partnership examples, mobile recording capability, community outcomes, leadership credibility.
- **Best CTA:** “Start a Partner Conversation.”
- **Content needed:** Workshop/residency/mobile recording/showcase options, partner needs, inquiry details.
- **Interaction that may help:** Partner inquiry panel with selectable interests and mailto/form fallback.
- **Desired feeling:** “DSG is credible, warm, and ready for a real conversation.”

## 7. Volunteer / community ally

- **Likely device:** Phone.
- **Emotional state:** Generous, curious, uncertain where they fit.
- **Main question:** “How can I help if I am not donating or joining a program?”
- **Objections/friction:** No visible volunteer path, unclear needs, no contact route.
- **Proof needed:** Community-centered language, practical help categories, transparent contact process.
- **Best CTA:** “Stay Connected” or “Contact DSG.”
- **Content needed:** Volunteer/community ally options once founder-approved.
- **Interaction that may help:** Stay-connected module with “I want to help” option or contact link.
- **Desired feeling:** “There is a meaningful way to support this community.”
